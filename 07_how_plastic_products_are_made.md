# Lesson 07: How Plastic Products Are Made

Plastic is useful because it can be shaped into products at scale.

The material starts as resin, often in pellet form.

Manufacturing turns that material into objects:

```text
bottles
caps
films
containers
pipes
cases
foam
fibers
```

## Why Care About Manufacturing?

Because product shape is not separate from material choice.

A plastic may be good for one process and bad for another.

Designers must think about:

```text
material
shape
process
cost
speed
defects
recycling
```

This lesson gives the first map of common plastic manufacturing methods.

## Step 1: Pellets To Product

Many plastic products begin as pellets.

Simple picture:

```text
pellets -> heat -> soften or melt -> shape -> cool -> product
```

Thermoplastics fit this pattern especially well because they can soften and flow
when heated.

The manufacturing process controls the final shape.

It can also affect the final properties.

## Step 2: Injection Molding

Injection molding makes parts by pushing melted plastic into a mold.

Simple flow:

```text
pellets melt
melt is injected into mold
plastic cools
part is removed
```

Common products:

```text
bottle caps
toy bricks
electronic housings
containers
automotive parts
```

Injection molding is good for making many copies of a precise shape.

The mold can be expensive, but each part can be fast once production is running.

## Step 3: Extrusion

Extrusion pushes melted plastic through a shaped opening called a die.

Simple flow:

```text
pellets melt
melt is pushed through die
continuous shape comes out
shape cools
```

Common products:

```text
pipes
tubes
films
sheets
profiles
wire coating
```

Extrusion is good for continuous shapes.

If the same cross-section continues for a long distance, extrusion may make
sense.

## Step 4: Blow Molding

Blow molding makes hollow objects.

Simple idea:

```text
hot plastic tube or preform
air pressure expands it
plastic touches mold walls
shape cools
```

Common products:

```text
drink bottles
detergent bottles
milk jugs
containers
```

The key idea is that air pressure helps form the inside volume.

That is why blow molding is important for bottles.

## Step 5: Thermoforming

Thermoforming starts with a plastic sheet.

The sheet is heated and pulled or pressed into shape.

Simple flow:

```text
sheet heats
sheet softens
sheet forms over mold
sheet cools
extra material is trimmed
```

Common products:

```text
food trays
clamshell packaging
cups
blister packs
thin containers
```

Thermoforming is common for packaging because it can make thin shapes quickly.

## Step 6: Film Blowing

Thin plastic bags and films are often made through film processes.

One common method is blown film extrusion.

Simple idea:

```text
melted plastic exits a circular die
air inflates it into a bubble
bubble cools
film is flattened and wound
```

This is how very thin flexible films can be produced continuously.

The process affects film thickness, strength, and directionality.

## Step 7: Foaming

Foamed plastics contain many gas pockets.

Simple model:

```text
plastic + gas bubbles -> lightweight foam
```

Foaming can create:

```text
cushioning
insulation
lightweight structure
impact protection
```

Examples:

```text
foam packaging
insulation boards
foam trays
cushioning materials
```

Foam is often mostly air by volume.

That is why it can be light but bulky.

## Step 8: Process Shapes Design

The manufacturing method affects design choices.

Injection molded parts need features that can leave the mold.

Extruded parts usually have a continuous profile.

Blow molded parts are hollow.

Thermoformed parts are often thin-walled.

Film products are flexible and thin.

Simple model:

```text
process limits shape
shape influences material choice
material influences performance
```

Good plastic design connects all three.

## Step 9: Three Quick Questions

Question:

```text
Which process is common for making precise repeated parts like caps or housings?
```

Answer:

```text
injection molding
```

Question:

```text
Which process is important for hollow bottles?
```

Answer:

```text
blow molding
```

Question:

```text
Why is extrusion useful for pipes and sheets?
```

Answer:

```text
it makes continuous shapes
```

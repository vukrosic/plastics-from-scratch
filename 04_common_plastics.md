# Lesson 04: Common Plastics

Plastic is a family of materials.

This lesson introduces the common types you will see again and again.

You do not need to memorize every chemical detail.

The goal is to connect:

```text
plastic name -> common use -> useful property
```

## Why Care About Common Plastics?

Because everyday objects start to make more sense when you can identify the
material family.

A clear drink bottle is not the same material problem as a flexible grocery bag.

A milk jug is not the same material problem as a foam cup.

The product shape, feel, and recycling pathway all depend on what kind of
plastic it is.

## Step 1: PET

PET stands for:

```text
polyethylene terephthalate
```

Common uses:

```text
drink bottles
food containers
polyester fibers
```

Why it is useful:

```text
clear
strong
lightweight
good barrier for many packaging uses
```

PET is common in beverage bottles because it can be clear, tough, and shaped
into thin-walled containers.

Important idea:

```text
PET is not just bottle plastic
polyester fabric is also PET-family material
```

## Step 2: HDPE

HDPE stands for:

```text
high-density polyethylene
```

Common uses:

```text
milk jugs
detergent bottles
shampoo bottles
some pipes
storage containers
```

Why it is useful:

```text
tough
chemically resistant
less flexible than LDPE
often cloudy or opaque
```

The "high-density" part means the polymer chains can pack more closely than in
low-density polyethylene.

That closer packing helps make HDPE stiffer and stronger than LDPE.

## Step 3: PVC

PVC stands for:

```text
polyvinyl chloride
```

Common uses:

```text
pipes
cable insulation
flooring
some building products
```

Why it is useful:

```text
durable
chemically resistant
can be rigid or flexible with additives
```

PVC is a good example of why additives matter.

Rigid PVC pipes and flexible cable insulation are not the same product feel.

The polymer family is related, but additives and processing change the result.

## Step 4: LDPE

LDPE stands for:

```text
low-density polyethylene
```

Common uses:

```text
plastic bags
films
squeeze bottles
some wraps
```

Why it is useful:

```text
flexible
soft
good for thin films
moisture resistant
```

LDPE has more chain branching than HDPE.

Branching makes it harder for chains to pack tightly.

That helps explain why LDPE is usually softer and more flexible.

## Step 5: PP

PP stands for:

```text
polypropylene
```

Common uses:

```text
bottle caps
food containers
living hinges
car parts
fibers
```

Why it is useful:

```text
lightweight
tough
fatigue resistant
good heat resistance for many packaging uses
```

PP is often used when the part needs to bend many times without snapping.

That is why you may see it in hinged caps and containers.

## Step 6: PS

PS stands for:

```text
polystyrene
```

Common uses:

```text
rigid disposable items
food trays
foam packaging
insulation foam
```

Why it is useful:

```text
can be rigid
can be foamed
lightweight when expanded
easy to shape
```

Polystyrene can be clear and rigid, or expanded into foam.

Foamed plastic is mostly air trapped in a polymer structure.

That is why foam packaging can be very light.

## Step 7: Other Plastics

The category "other" can include many materials.

Examples:

```text
polycarbonate
nylon
ABS
PLA
multi-layer materials
mixed plastics
```

This category is broad.

It does not mean the plastic is bad.

It means the product does not fit cleanly into the first six common resin code
categories.

## Step 8: Simple Comparison

Use this rough mental map:

```text
PET:
    clear bottles and food packaging

HDPE:
    tougher cloudy bottles and containers

PVC:
    pipes, flooring, cable insulation

LDPE:
    flexible films and bags

PP:
    caps, food containers, hinges

PS:
    rigid disposables and foam

Other:
    many specialty or mixed plastics
```

This is not enough to identify every object perfectly.

But it gives you the first useful map.

## Step 9: Three Quick Questions

Question:

```text
Which plastic is common in clear drink bottles?
```

Answer:

```text
PET
```

Question:

```text
Which plastic is commonly used for flexible films and bags?
```

Answer:

```text
LDPE
```

Question:

```text
Why is the "other" category broad?
```

Answer:

```text
it can include many specialty, mixed, or multi-layer plastics
```
